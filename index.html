Measuring Motion

Motion and change capture time as a distance traveled. We use sundial, pendulum, watch hand to anthropomorphize time into a tangible measurement, but our modern perception of time is not universal. It has developed towards greater standardization and precision to create congruity. Like any system design the framing informs the visual representation. While a clock affords a cycles, a count represents the progression. These digital time keepers compare these two approached first shows time as colors moving in a cycle and the second counts the uniqueness of each moment in a progression.

Revolve

This timepiece cycles every day, the color of each arc set by the horizon line. It gradients from first to last light. A full circle marks the progress of daylight. As days lengthen into summer or shorten into winter the diameter responds. Like Marey's chronophotgraphy, it shows a relative trajectory of motion: each shade relative to the prior and the next.
